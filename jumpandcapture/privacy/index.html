Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 20, 2026

Jump & Capture (“the App”) is published by Maverick Codebase. This Privacy Policy explains what information the App handles and how it is used when you play on iOS or Android.

Information we collect

  • Profile details you choose, such as your display name and portrait character.
  • Gameplay statistics stored on your device, including results by mode (vs AI, Pass & Play, Local, and Online), win streaks, lifetime XP earned across game modes, and scenario progress such as levels cleared and stars earned.
  • An unfinished vs AI or Pass & Play match may be saved on your device so you can Continue later.
  • A daily play streak — which calendar days you have played, plus your current and best streak — used to show the streak calendar on your device.
  • Settings preferences such as sound volume, music volume, haptics, and whether optional notifications are enabled.

How we use information

  • To personalize your profile and in-game appearance.
  • To show your statistics inside the App.
  • To remember your audio, haptic, and notification preferences between sessions.
  • To schedule optional local play reminders on your device when you turn notifications on in Settings.

Local multiplayer (Same Wi‑Fi)

  • On iOS, Local play discovers nearby devices on the same Wi‑Fi network using Apple’s local-network and peer-discovery technologies.
  • On Android, Same Wi‑Fi play uses Android Nearby Connections so two devices on the same local network can find each other and play.
  • Your chosen display name and character portrait may be visible to nearby players so they can invite you to a match.
  • Match data is exchanged directly between devices. Maverick Codebase does not receive or store these local sessions on our servers.

Online play, leaderboards & Firebase

  • Online Quick Match and Invite a Friend use Google Firebase (Firestore) to pair players and relay match moves while you play. Matches may be against players on iOS or Android.
  • On iOS, Apple’s Game Center is used to sign in for online features. Apple shares your Game Center identity (such as your player ID and Game Center alias) as described in Apple’s privacy policy.
  • On Android, Google Play Games is used to sign in and to sync your personal lifetime XP.
  • During a match, your chosen in-game display name and character bead are sent to your opponent so each side can see who they are playing.
  • “Invite a Friend” room codes and active match state are stored temporarily in Google Firebase (Firestore) so devices can find each other and exchange moves. These records are temporary and are not used as a permanent gameplay archive.
  • The online leaderboard stores your cumulative lifetime XP and a small public Firebase (Firestore) profile — your chosen display name and character bead — so other players can see your portrait on the shared cross-platform leaderboard. XP is earned from scenarios, vs AI, Local, and online matches.
  • We do not require a separate Maverick Codebase account. An anonymous Firebase session may be created automatically so matchmaking and leaderboard portraits can sync.

Notifications

  • If you enable notifications in Settings, the App asks your device for permission to show alerts (and sounds on iOS).
  • We use local notifications only — an optional daily reminder to play, which may mention your current play streak. Reminders are scheduled on your device by the App; we do not use a push notification server and we do not send marketing messages.
  • Notifications are never required to play. You can turn them off at any time in the App’s Settings or in system settings, and any scheduled reminders are removed.

Where data is stored

  • Your profile, statistics, daily play streak, scenario progress, and settings are stored locally on your device using standard on-device app storage.
  • Online matchmaking, match relay, and leaderboard display profiles (name and character) are stored in Google Firebase (Firestore).
  • We do not require a Maverick Codebase account to play. Online sign-in uses Game Center on iOS and Play Games on Android; matchmaking and leaderboard portraits use Firebase.

Advertising

  • The free version of the App shows ads from Google AdMob, including banners on some menu screens and occasional full-screen ads between matches.
  • To serve and measure ads, Google’s Mobile Ads SDK may collect device identifiers (such as the advertising identifier), coarse location, and app-usage and diagnostic data, as described in Google’s policies.
  • On iOS, when you first start playing, the system may ask for permission to track you (App Tracking Transparency). If you allow it, you may see more relevant (personalized) ads; if you decline, you still see ads, but non‑personalized ones.
  • You can buy Remove Ads — a one-time, non-consumable in-app purchase — to remove all banner and full-screen ads. Once purchased, no ads are requested or shown.
  • Learn more about how Google uses data from apps that use its services at https://policies.google.com/technologies/partner-sites.

Third-party services

  • Apple App Store / StoreKit (iOS) and Google Play Billing (Android) process purchases when you buy Remove Ads. We receive only whether the purchase is active, not your payment details.
  • Apple Game Center (iOS) provides online sign-in identity under Apple’s privacy policy.
  • Google Play Games (Android) provides optional sign-in and XP sync under Google’s privacy policy.
  • Google Firebase (Firestore and Authentication) stores online matchmaking, match relay, and public leaderboard display profiles under Google’s privacy policy.
  • Google AdMob provides advertising as described above under Google’s privacy policy.
  • Apple or Google may collect diagnostic and analytics data according to your device settings and their respective privacy policies.

Children’s privacy

  • The App is a general-audience game and is not directed at children under 13.
  • We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided personal information, contact us and we will help you remove locally stored App data by uninstalling the App or clearing / resetting its data.

Your choices

  • You can change your profile name and character at any time in the App.
  • You can adjust or disable sound, music, haptics, and notifications in Settings.
  • On iOS, you can decline tracking when asked (or change it later in Settings ▸ Privacy & Security ▸ Tracking); you will still see non‑personalized ads.
  • You can remove all ads with the one-time Remove Ads purchase, and restore it on a new device with Restore Purchases.
  • You can decline local-network / nearby / notification permissions when asked; Local play and reminders simply will not work until permission is granted.
  • Deleting the App removes locally stored profile, statistics, and settings data from your device.

Changes to this policy

  • We may update this Privacy Policy when we add features such as ads, online play, or account sign-in.
  • The “Last updated” date at the top of this page will change when the policy is revised.
